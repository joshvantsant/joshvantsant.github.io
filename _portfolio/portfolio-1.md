### RoboCup — Autonomous Arena Robot
**Third Year**
*Tools: Arduino · Embedded C++ · Custom PCB · CAD*

As part of a team of three, I designed and built a fully autonomous robot for 
a RoboCup-style arena competition. The robot navigated an arena independently 
to locate and collect small metallic weights, requiring tight integration of 
hardware, electronics, and software.

- **Custom PCB** — designed and fabricated a purpose-built board integrating 
  sensors, actuators, and the Arduino microcontroller
- **Multi-sensor control system** — implemented in Arduino C++, handling 
  navigation and collection logic autonomously
- **Full mechanical design** — chassis designed in CAD and physically assembled 
  and tested

### Robotics — Manipulators & Autonomous Navigation (ENMT482)
**Fourth Year**
*Topics: Kinematics · Jacobians · Bayes Filters · SLAM · Path Planning*

A project-based course covering the two pillars of modern robotics. The 
manipulator section covered forward/inverse kinematics, Jacobian construction, 
and end-effector path planning. The mobile robotics section introduced 
probabilistic sensor fusion (Kalman and particle filters), SLAM, and autonomous 
navigation — the mathematical foundations underlying modern mobile manipulator 
platforms. Work was carried out in both simulation and with real industrial 
and research robots.

### Embedded Systems — Wacky Racers (ENCE461)
**Fourth Year**
*Tools: Altium Designer · Embedded C · Surface Mount Assembly*

A project-based course centred on designing, assembling, and programming a 
microcontroller-based remote-controlled vehicle using multilayer PCBs. Working 
in a team of four, the project covered PCB design in Altium Designer, 
surface mount component assembly, embedded firmware for motor and peripheral 
control, and hands-on hardware debugging with oscilloscopes and logic analysers.
